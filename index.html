The world's biggest party!

Welcome to the Magic of Brazilian Carnival

Carnival in Brazil is an annual festival held the Friday afternoon before Ash Wednesday at noon, which marks the beginning of Lent. It's known for elaborate parades, vibrant costumes, samba music, and an atmosphere of joy that takes over the entire country.

1. Samba Schools Parade

The heart of Rio's Carnival is the Samba School Parade at the Sambadrome. Each school prepares all year for their 80-minute performance with thousands of dancers, elaborate floats, and original samba songs.

2. Street Parties (Blocos)

Throughout Brazilian cities, hundreds of street parties called "blocos" take place. These are free events where people dance to live music, wear costumes, and celebrate together in the streets.

3. Elaborate Costumes

Carnival costumes range from simple homemade outfits to incredibly elaborate designs with feathers, sequins, and bright colors. Many costumes take months to create and can weigh up to 30kg!

5. King Momo Tradition

Each year, a "King Momo" is chosen to symbolically rule over Carnival. Traditionally a tall, heavy man (representing excess before Lent), he receives the keys to the city and leads the celebrations.
